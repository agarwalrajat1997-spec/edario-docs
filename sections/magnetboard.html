What Magnetboard is Your final-mile scheduling workspace
In Progress

Magnetboard is the “final-mile” workspace where a school reviews the schedule Edario generated (or the current draft), moves sections around, resolves conflicts/warnings, validates quality through reports, and fine-tunes exact section details using Edit Section.

Use Magnetboard to
  • Review the generated schedule (or your latest draft)
  • Place / re-place section cards into period slots (drag-and-drop)
  • Resolve Conflicts and reduce Warnings
  • Validate schedule quality using Reports (matched requests, conflict matrix, balancing)
  • Open Edit Section to adjust course/teacher/room/period(s)/days/quarters and manage enrolled students
Designed like a digital board: cards (sections) + grid (periods) + views (Teacher/Classroom/Student). Schools iterate here until conflicts are resolved, warnings are acceptable, and reports confirm quality.

Recommended workflow The smart order schools follow
In Progress

Magnetboard works best when schools follow a consistent flow: start with headline indicators, place what’s unplaced, fix conflicts, reduce warnings, validate with reports, then fine-tune via Edit Section.

Best-practice playbook
  1. Start with health indicators
    • Check Match %
    • Note Conflicts and Warnings counts
  2. Place what’s unplaced first
    • Use links like Unplaced sections
    • Use search to find the teacher/course quickly
    • Drag cards into open + slots
  3. Fix hard conflicts (blockers)
    • Click See all and work Conflicts first
    • Resolve teacher/room double-bookings via move or Edit Section
  4. Reduce warnings (quality improvements)
    • Warnings may be non-fatal but can indicate poor distribution/quality
    • Fix what matters most to the school’s priorities
  5. Validate using Reports
    • Schedule Summary: low matched courses, seat issues, unplaced sections
    • Conflict Matrix: biggest course clashes
    • Section Balancing: period/day distribution + lab overload
    • Student Summary: final student-level audit
  6. Fine-tune exact section definitions
    • Add co-teachers
    • Adjust days/quarters
    • Add or change periods
    • Manually add/remove students (and maintain inclusion subsections)
  7. Ignore only what you accept
    • Use Ignored Conflicts / Ignored Warnings for intentional exceptions
    • Keeps remaining list meaningful
