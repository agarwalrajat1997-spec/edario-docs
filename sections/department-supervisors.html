Review departments Add / edit / remove as needed
In Progress

Clean department naming keeps scheduling organized and enables safe delegation. In this step, you’ll create the departments your school needs, rename anything inconsistent, and remove departments you don’t want to use.

Where to find it. Scheduling Wizard → Step 4: Manage Departments. Make sure you’re working in the correct school year (year dropdown at top-left, e.g., 2026–2027).
1.1 — Page layout (what you’ll see)

You’ll see a Departments list. Each department appears as a card with:

  • Department name (e.g., Mathematics, Science, Fine Arts)
  • Edit icon (pencil) next to the name (rename/edit)
  • Delete icon (×) on the top-right of the card (remove)
  • A Supervisor chip showing either a person or No Department Supervisor
  • A link: Assign a Department Supervisor (optional delegation)

Primary actions on the page:

  • Add New Department (creates a new department card)
  • Continue to Data Cleaning (bottom-right) to move forward
Tip. Use department names that match what your school already uses in course catalogs/reporting.
Replace manage-departments-layout.mp4 with your real filename.
1.2 — Add a new department
  1. Click Add New Department.
  2. Enter the department name (e.g., Mathematics, Science, Fine Arts).
  3. Save/confirm — the new department appears as a new card.
Tip. Start with the set of departments your school uses for reporting. You can always add more later.
Replace manage-departments-add-department.mp4 with your real filename.
1.3 — Rename / edit a department
  1. On the department card, click the pencil (edit) icon next to the name.
  2. Update the name.
  3. Save/confirm.
Good practice. Keep naming consistent and avoid duplicates (e.g., “Math” vs “Mathematics”).
Replace manage-departments-rename-department.mp4 with your real filename.
1.4 — Remove a department
  1. Click the × (delete) icon on the department card.
  2. Confirm deletion (if prompted).
Good practice. Only delete departments you’re sure you don’t need — especially if your school data or workflows reference them.
Replace manage-departments-delete-department.mp4 with your real filename.

Assign department supervisors (optional) Share scheduling responsibility safely
In Progress

Assign supervisors if department heads will validate offerings and eligibility — or if you want departments to “own” their course planning. If you leave a supervisor blank, Admins retain full scheduling control.

2.1 — Assign a Department Supervisor (optional)
  1. On a department card, click Assign a Department Supervisor.
  2. Choose a supervisor from the dropdown (examples: Admin, Edario; Agarwal, Rajat).
  3. The selection appears on the card as the Supervisor chip.
  4. If you don’t want delegation, leave it as No Department Supervisor.
Reminder. Leaving a supervisor blank means the Admin team keeps full control of scheduling for that department.
Replace manage-departments-assign-supervisor.mp4 with your real filename.
2.2 — “Add new Supervisor” (create supervisor account first)

If the department dropdown shows Add new Supervisor, you’ll need to create the user account first.

  1. Go to Settings → Users.
  2. Find the Department Supervisors section.
  3. Click Add New User.
  4. Create the supervisor account (name/email + required fields).
  5. Return to this step and assign the supervisor from the dropdown.
Note. Settings → Users is organized by role: Admins, Department Supervisors, Counselors.
Replace settings-users-add-supervisor.mp4 with your real filename.
2.3 — Recommended workflow
  1. Create all departments your school needs.
  2. Create supervisor accounts (if delegating) in Settings → Users.
  3. Assign supervisors to the relevant departments.
  4. Click Continue to Data Cleaning to proceed.
Optional. School profile fields are in Settings → Account (School Name, School Code). Click Save Changes to apply.
