Build the schedule structure Add days, periods, lunches, and rotating periods
In Progress

On Schedule Structure, you define each day template: periods on the left, lunches in the middle, and rotating period blocks on the right.

2.1 — Add cycle days (Day tabs)
  1. A “day cycle” is how many unique days your schedule uses before it repeats back to Day 1.
  2. Look at the tabs along the top (Day 1, Day 2, etc.). These tabs represent the days in your cycle.
  3. Click “+ Add Day” to create the next day tab. Each click adds exactly one more day (Day 3, Day 4, and so on).
  4. Keep adding days until the tabs match what your school actually runs. Example: a 5-day cycle should end at Day 5. A 6-day cycle should end at Day 6.
  5. When your day tabs are correct, click into each Day tab and build that day’s periods (next step). Some schools keep the same period times on every day, but others change times, lunches, or rotation mappings depending on the day.
Pro Tip. If you’re not sure, start with a 5-day cycle and confirm with the school. Adding Day 6 later is easy and won’t undo your existing setup.
2.2 — Add and manage periods (time slots)
  1. Period rows are the building blocks of a day. Each row represents one real time slot in the bell schedule (e.g., “8:30–9:20”).
  2. Select the correct Day tab first (Day 1, Day 2, etc.). Periods are created per day.
  3. Click “+ Add a Period” to create a new period row. Keep going until the number of rows matches that day.
  4. To reorder, drag the grab handle (dotted icon) on the left side of a period row.
  5. Click into the time fields and enter the start/end times for that row.
  6. “Include in Match?” controls whether a period can hold scheduled classes. Off = non-instructional time.
Pro Tip. Get the structure right first — details are easier once the day layout is correct.

2.3 — Add and map rotating periods (rotation labels)
  1. Rotating periods are labels. They do not create new time slots — your period rows are the real schedule.
  2. Add the labels you need using “+ Add a Rotating Period” (e.g., 1–8 or 1–9).
  3. For each Day tab, drag a rotating tile into the “Drop a rotating period here” box in each instructional period row.
  4. Repeat for all days in the cycle. Day 2 can look different from Day 1 in rotating schedules.
  5. Quick check: each instructional period row should have a rotating tile (unless intentionally blank).
Pro Tip. Finish one day completely, then move to the next. Scan for any empty mapping boxes.
Replace schedule-structure-rotating-periods.mp4 with your real filename.
2.4 — Add lunches (optional)
  1. Lunches are optional. Add them only if your school has one lunch time or multiple lunch waves.
  2. Click “+ Add a Lunch” to create a lunch card (Lunch 1, Lunch 2, Lunch 3).
  3. Rename the lunch card (edit/pen icon) to match the school naming.
  4. Enter lunch start and end times in the time boxes.
  5. Edario will reflect lunch tags on the corresponding period cards once times are set.
  6. Final check: names are clear, times are valid, and the lunch tag appears where expected.
Pro Tip. Keep lunch names short and consistent. Ensure lunch times fit inside the intended lunch window.

Once you are happy with your day tabs, period rows, times, and any rotating labels, you are ready to submit.

1.) Do a quick final scan: every day in the cycle is added, period times look correct, and any instructional periods you want scheduled are marked “Include in Match”.

2.) If your school uses rotating periods, make sure each instructional period row has a rotating label placed (no empty “drop” boxes).

3.) When everything looks right, click the green “Submit Schedule Structure” button to confirm and save.
